# Weekly Coffee Report

Copenhagen ran away with the week — three stops, all solid, anchored by a La Cabra V60 that tasted like peach syrup in a cup (a flat 10, no notes). The outright best cup was tied between that La Cabra and Prufrock's Ethiopia Guji pour-over in London, all blueberry and jasmine in a quiet corner with fast wifi — basically the platonic ideal of a working café. The low point, and it was genuinely low, was a €5.50 cold brew at Café Nowhere in Paris that the notes describe as "burnt tire steeped in tap water" — hard pass, never again. Average spend landed right at €4.50, and the splurges mostly earned their keep: both €5.20 filters were perfect, while the priciest cup of the week (that cold brew) turned out to be the worst by a country mile. The pattern was unmistakable — filter is where it's at right now, with both 10s coming from pour-overs, milk drinks sitting reliably in the 7–8 range, and the one cold brew experiment ending in tears. If there's a takeaway, it's this: when a café's whole identity leans on vibes, check the cup before you commit.
